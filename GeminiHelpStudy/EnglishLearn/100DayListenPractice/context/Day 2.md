### Article A
**Title:** Managing Your Daily Tasks

**Text:** 
Good morning! Today, I want to talk about productivity. Many of us struggle to finish our daily tasks. A good strategy is to write down everything you need to do in the morning. For example, you can make a to-do list. However, it is important to prioritize the hardest tasks first. If you finish the difficult work early, you will feel much better. Meanwhile, you should avoid checking your phone too often. As a result, you can stay focused for longer periods. In other words, managing your time well reduces stress. How do you plan your day?

**Vocabulary Notes:**
*   **Productivity:** How efficiently you complete tasks.
*   **Struggle:** To try very hard to do something difficult.
*   **Strategy:** A clear plan of action to achieve a goal.
*   **Prioritize:** To put the most important things first.
*   **Reduces:** Makes something smaller or less intense.

**Speaking Phrases:**
*   Today, I want to talk about...
*   Many of us struggle to...
*   For example, you can...
*   It is important to...
*   How do you plan your day?

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. What is a recommended strategy for the morning?
2. Why should you do the hardest tasks first?
3. What happens when you avoid checking your phone?

### Article B (Semantic Variant)
**Title:** Improving Your Daily Efficiency

**Text:** 
Hello everyone! Let's discuss getting things done efficiently. A lot of people find it hard to complete their daily assignments. One effective method is to list all your required activities right after you wake up. You could create a task schedule, for instance. Nevertheless, it is crucial to tackle the most challenging items right away. Finishing the hardest job first gives you a great sense of relief. At the same time, try to stay away from your mobile device. Consequently, you are able to maintain your concentration longer. Simply put, good time management lowers anxiety. What is your routine?

**Key Differences From Article A:**
*   **"Productivity"** changed to **"getting things done efficiently"**.
*   **"Struggle"** was swapped with **"find it hard"**.
*   Connectors changed: **"However"** became **"Nevertheless"**, and **"As a result"** became **"Consequently"**.
*   **"Prioritize"** was explained as **"tackle the most challenging items"**.

**Vocabulary Expansion:**
*   **Efficiently:** Working well without wasting time or energy.
*   **Effective method:** A way of doing something that produces good results.
*   **Tackle:** To directly deal with a problem or task.
*   **Sense of relief:** A feeling of comfort when a difficulty is gone.
*   **Concentration:** The ability to focus your attention completely.
