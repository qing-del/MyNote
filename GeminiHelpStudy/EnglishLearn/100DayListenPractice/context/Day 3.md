### Article A

**Title:** Preparing for a Successful Presentation

**Text:** Good afternoon, everyone. Today, I want to share some tips for giving a great presentation in the workplace. First, it is crucial to know your audience before you start writing. If you understand what they need, you can tailor your message perfectly. For example, you can use simple charts instead of complex data. Moreover, you should practice your speech multiple times to build confidence. Consequently, you will speak more clearly and naturally during the real meeting. On the other hand, do not read directly from your slides. In other words, make eye contact to keep people engaged. Finally, always leave time for questions at the end. Does anyone want to share their own experiences?

**Vocabulary Notes:**

- **Crucial:** Extremely important or necessary.
- **Tailor:** To adjust something to suit a specific need or situation.
- **Multiple:** Many or several.
- **Confidence:** A feeling of belief in your own abilities.
- **Engaged:** Interested and paying close attention.

**Speaking Phrases:**

- Today, I want to share some tips for...
- First, it is crucial to...
- For example, you can...
- On the other hand...
- Does anyone want to share their own experiences?

**Comprehension Questions:**

1. Why is it important to know your audience before writing a presentation?
2. What happens when you practice your speech multiple times?
3. Why should a speaker make eye contact instead of reading from slides?

### Article B (Semantic Variant)

**Title:** Getting Ready for an Effective Speech

**Text:** Hello team. Let's talk about strategies for delivering an excellent speech at work. Initially, understanding who you are speaking to is essential before drafting your notes. When you know their expectations, you can adjust your content to fit perfectly. You could include clear visuals rather than confusing numbers, for instance. Furthermore, rehearse your talk several times to increase your self-assurance. As a result, your delivery will be smoother and more authentic when it matters most. Alternatively, avoid staring at your presentation screen while you talk. Simply put, look at your listeners to maintain their attention. Lastly, make sure to reserve a few minutes for a discussion afterwards. Who would like to discuss their past presentations?

**Key Differences From Article A:**

- **"Giving a great presentation"** changed to **"delivering an excellent speech"** to vary public speaking vocabulary.
- **"Tailor your message"** was swapped with **"adjust your content"**.
- Logical connectors changed: **"Moreover"** became **"Furthermore"**, and **"Consequently"** became **"As a result"**.
- **"Make eye contact"** was explained as **"look at your listeners"**.

**Vocabulary Expansion:**

- **Essential:** Absolutely necessary.
- **Adjust:** To change something slightly to make it work better.
- **Rehearse:** To practice a speech or performance before the real event.
- **Self-assurance:** Confidence in yourself and your abilities.
- **Authentic:** Real, genuine, and natural.
