### Day 5: Problem Solving

#### Article A
**Title:** Solving a Supply Problem

**Text:** 
Good morning, team. We have an unexpected problem with our new product design. The main supplier is completely out of stock for a necessary part. Therefore, we cannot finish manufacturing on time. We need to find a solution quickly. For example, we can contact alternative vendors in the city. However, their prices might be much higher. On the other hand, we could ask our clients for a short deadline extension. Let's brainstorm some ideas together today. What do you think is the best option?

**Vocabulary Notes:**
*   **Unexpected:** Surprising because it was not planned or thought of.
*   **Out of stock:** Not available to buy or use because it has all been sold.
*   **Manufacturing:** The process of making goods in large numbers.
*   **Alternative:** Something that can be used instead of another thing.
*   **Brainstorm:** To discuss and produce a lot of ideas quickly to solve a problem.

**Speaking Phrases:**
*   We have an unexpected problem with...
*   Therefore, we cannot...
*   We need to find a solution quickly.
*   Let's brainstorm some ideas together today.
*   What do you think is the best option?

**Comprehension Questions:**
1. Why is the company unable to finish manufacturing on time?
2. What is a negative aspect of using alternative vendors?
3. What is the second proposed solution?

#### Article B (Semantic Variant)
**Title:** Fixing a Material Shortage

**Text:** 
Hello everyone. We are facing an unforeseen issue regarding our latest item blueprint. Our primary distributor has no inventory left for a required component. Because of this, we are unable to complete production on schedule. We must discover a fix immediately. We could reach out to different suppliers in town, for instance. Nevertheless, their costs could be significantly more expensive. Alternatively, we might request a brief delay from our buyers. Let's generate some options collaboratively this afternoon. What is your preferred choice?

**Key Differences From Article A:**
*   **"Unexpected problem"** changed to **"unforeseen issue"**.
*   **"Out of stock"** was swapped with **"no inventory left"**, and **"necessary part"** became **"required component"**.
*   Connectors changed: **"Therefore"** became **"Because of this"**, and **"On the other hand"** became **"Alternatively"**.
*   **"Brainstorm some ideas"** was explained as **"generate some options collaboratively"**.

**Vocabulary Expansion:**
*   **Unforeseen:** Not expected or predicted.
*   **Inventory:** The amount of goods or materials kept in a warehouse or store.
*   **Component:** One of the parts that make up a machine or system.
*   **Production:** The process of making or growing things to be sold.
*   **Discover a fix:** To find a way to solve a problem or repair something.
