📝 句型知识点：条件句的倒装 (Inversion in Conditional Sentences)
在正式的写作中，为了简洁或强调，可以省略 if 并将 should, were, 或 had 提前，形成倒装。
 * 省略 if + 倒装结构：
   * Should + 主语 + 动词原形... (表示可能性较小的条件)
   * Were + 主语 + (to V / 宾语)... (表示与事实相反或将来不可能)
   * Had + 主语 + 动词过去分词... (表示与过去事实相反)
 * 示例：
   * 原句： If you should need any help, call me.
   * 倒装句： Should you need any help, call me. (如果你需要任何帮助，请给我打电话。)
 * 原文拓展句 (RC C): Had they taken earlier measures, the financial crisis might not have happened. (如果他们早点采取措施，金融危机可能就不会发生。)

Reading Comprehension A: 气候变化与经济 (Climate Change and the Global Economy)
Climate change is rapidly becoming one of the most significant challenges to global economic stability. Its effects are often seen in extreme weather events that can immediately damage infrastructure and interrupt supply chains. The agricultural sector, in particular, is highly vulnerable to unpredictable weather patterns, causing crop failures and food scarcity.
Furthermore, prolonged shifts in climate patterns will fluctuate the availability of key resources like water, forcing industries to adopt expensive new technologies or relocation plans. Governments and businesses are increasingly forced to allocate large amounts of capital to mitigation and adaptation, funds which otherwise could contribute to growth. While some argue that the transition to green energy will stimulate innovation, the short-term financial burdens mean many companies will suffer losses before the benefits diminish the overall cost.

Reading Comprehension B: 习得性无助 (Learned Helplessness)
Learned helplessness is a psychological tendency where an individual, having suffered repeated painful or adverse situations, comes to believe they have no control over their situation and ceases to try to overcome it—even when opportunities to escape become available. This theory, initially developed through animal studies, has important implications for human behavior, especially in areas like education and mental health.
When students repeatedly fail, for instance, they may start to interpret new challenges as insurmountable (不可逾越的), which can severely diminish their academic performance. Therapists urge individuals to confront this negative belief system by seeking small, manageable successes. By experiencing small victories, individuals can slowly rebuild their sense of control and self-resilience, replacing the learned inaction with an initiative for positive change. The key is recognizing the thought pattern and understanding that one's current circumstances are not necessarily permanent.

Reading Comprehension C: 新技术与传统工作 (New Tech vs. Conventional Jobs)
The introduction of highly sophisticated automated systems into many workplaces has sparked a heated debate regarding the future of conventional labor. Critics exaggerate the speed at which robots will substitute human workers, often ignoring the fact that new technology simultaneously creates entirely new job categories. History shows that technology often shifts, rather than eliminates, employment; the typewriter did not end writing, but changed its form.
However, the nature of work is changing. Routine tasks are increasingly being automated, leaving a stronger demand for skills related to creativity, critical thinking, and social flexibility. Unlike their immediate predecessors, today's workers must be prepared to adopt continuous learning to remain competitive. Economists urge policymakers to confront the coming transition by investing heavily in retraining programs and education initiatives to ensure that the workforce is not left vulnerable during this ongoing transformation. Had they taken earlier measures, the transition might have been smoother.
